# Blog Disclosure And Framing Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing posts that involve AI assistance,
research-themed prototypes, scientific language, public repositories, external
links, provisional work, or domain-sensitive material.

This checklist is an authoring aid. It does not claim that a post has been
scientifically audited, independently validated, peer reviewed, or approved by
domain experts.

## Article Identity

- The title says what the post is about without overstating the result.
- The front matter description is one direct sentence.
- The URL slug is stable, lowercase, and descriptive.
- The opening paragraph sets the scope of the post plainly.

## Claims And Evidence

- Claims about what Ryan built, tested, wrote, or observed are supported by the
  post, a linked artifact, or a reviewed source.
- Interpretive statements are separated from facts.
- Provisional, exploratory, unfinished, or AI-assisted work is marked clearly.
- The post does not imply validation, peer review, experimental support,
  production readiness, scientific correctness, clinical relevance, safety
  relevance, or operational suitability unless separately reviewed and sourced.
- If the post discusses limitations, failures, or caveats, they are not hidden
  at the bottom when they are central to understanding the work.

## AI Assistance

- If AI assistance materially shaped the work, the post says so plainly.
- The post distinguishes AI-assisted workflow artifacts from conventionally
  authored or independently validated work.
- The post does not imply that generated code, reports, citations, analyses, or
  scientific-style artifacts are reliable simply because they are polished.
- Any discussion of model behavior stays grounded in what was actually observed
  or linked.

## Scientific Or Domain-Sensitive Material

- Scientific, biomedical, chemistry, biology, physics, protein, data-analysis,
  or domain-specific content is framed as provisional unless a reviewed source
  supports a stronger claim.
- Research-themed prototypes are described as software, interface, workflow,
  documentation, or methodology artifacts unless a stronger description has been
  explicitly reviewed.
- The post does not present prototype outputs as validated findings.
- The post does not invite scientific, biomedical, clinical, engineering,
  safety-relevant, or operational decision-making from provisional content.

## Provider Guardrail

- The post does not introduce provider-specific or product-specific content
  covered by the project guardrails unless a future task explicitly authorizes
  and reviews it.
- External tool, platform, or provider references are included only when they
  are relevant, accurate, and claim-safe.

## Links And Public Surface

- All public links resolve to the intended destination.
- Link text is descriptive and does not overstate what the destination proves.
- GitHub, app, dataset, article, or demo links are factual and current enough
  for the post.
- Private notes, local-only paths, credentials, tokens, and unpublished account
  details are absent.

## Layout And Accessibility

- Preview the post on desktop and mobile.
- Confirm there is no page-level horizontal overflow.
- Confirm long links, tables, code blocks, and images wrap or scroll cleanly.
- Confirm headings are in a sensible order.
- Confirm keyboard focus remains visible for interactive elements.
- Confirm the theme switcher still defaults to System and the page remains
  readable in light and dark modes.
